# Governance

glamfire is an open‑source project under the **glamworks** organization. Governance is
deliberately lightweight now and will formalize as the community grows — transparently,
in the open.

## Principles

- **Open and honest.** Decisions, roadmap (as issues), and "current reality" are public.
  We do not market vaporware.
- **Meritocratic.** Influence is earned through sustained, high‑quality contribution —
  code, review, docs, research, community.
- **Spec‑led.** [`SPEC.md`](SPEC.md) is the product contract. Material changes to it go
  through an RFC (see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)).

## Roles

- **Contributors** — anyone who opens an issue or PR. Welcome by default.
- **Maintainers** — review and merge, triage issues, cut releases, uphold the
  no‑shims / human‑verified‑DONE bar. Added by existing maintainers on a track record
  of merged, verified work.
- **Stewards** — current org owners; responsible for the trademark, the meme‑coin
  separation policy, security disclosures, and final say where consensus fails. The
  steward role is intended to shrink over time as a maintainer council forms.

## Decisions

- Day‑to‑day: lazy consensus on issues/PRs — propose, and if no blocking objection in a
  reasonable window, proceed.
- Substantial/interface/spec changes: RFC issue, public discussion, maintainer sign‑off.
- Disagreements escalate to maintainers, then stewards, in the open.

## Releases

Semantic versioning (patch included), version in the product's output, every release
committed, pushed, and tagged. See [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md) §6 and `/ship`.

## Money & tokens

The **$GLAM / $GLAMFIRE** community tokens are **strictly separate** from the software
and its governance. The software never depends on them, and no contributor is ever
required to hold or transact a token. Tokens are advertised only once live. See
[`research/14-crypto-legal-risk.md`](research/14-crypto-legal-risk.md).
