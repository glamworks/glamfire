# Mission

**Keep the last mile of AI open, and in the hands of the people who do the work.**

Intelligence became cheap. Within a single year, open models reached — and on the broad
middle of everyday work, surpassed — the frontier, at a fraction of the cost, free to
self‑host. That should have been a liberation. Instead it is becoming a new dependency.

The model was never the lock‑in. The **harness** is: the context a model is fed, the
routing that picks it, the tool‑calls shaped to its grammar, the prompts tuned to its
quirks, and the surfaces where work happens. Building that harness takes scarce, costly
talent — so most teams rent a frontier contract instead. And the labs are moving the
harness *inside the team*, absorbing the uncodified context that is a company's real
edge, until the firm is renting its own brain back, forever, with no way out. That is
the context war: the fight is no longer over model quality — it is over who sits
closest to your context.

glamfire exists to make the opposite future the easy one:

- **Own your context.** Local‑first, portable, exportable. If you leave, you leave with
  your brain.
- **Route your intelligence.** Intelligence is now a commodity market; glamfire is the
  buyer's side of it. The cheapest model that can actually do the task does it; the
  frontier must earn its escalations.
- **One harness, every model.** Tested adapters turn any model into a working agent — no
  brain in a jar, no rewrite to switch.
- **Open to the builders.** The harness‑talent shortage is the opening of the decade.
  We build the harness in the open so anyone can own their last mile, not just the few
  companies that can afford to hire for it.

We measure success not by tokens billed, but by **how many teams stop renting their
brain** — and by how good, cheap, and free their AI gets as a result.
