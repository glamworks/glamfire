# Positioning & messaging

From [`../research/19-positioning-messaging.md`](../research/19-positioning-messaging.md).

## Category we name

**The open harness.** glamfire isn't "another agent framework." It is the *harness for
the last mile* — the layer that makes cheap open intelligence actually usable while you
keep your context. Own your last mile.

## North-star positioning statement

> For builders and teams who know open models are now good enough but can't switch,
> **glamfire** is the open, self-owned harness that routes each task to the cheapest
> capable model and keeps your context in your hands — unlike frontier assistants that
> embed in your team and rent your own brain back to you.

## Taglines (lead with one, keep them consistent)

- *The open harness for the last mile of AI.*
- *Own your context. Route your intelligence. Never rent your brain back.*
- *Cheap intelligence is here. Own the harness.*
- *Your brain shouldn't be a subscription.*

## Words we own / words we avoid

- **Own:** harness, last mile, own your context, route, center vs. edge of distribution,
  cheapest capable model, rent your brain back, no brain in a jar.
- **Avoid:** "AGI", "magic", "10x", vague "powered by AI", anything we can't show.

## Proof pillars (mechanism → consequence → proof)

1. **Center/edge routing** → cheap by default, frontier only when needed → show the
   route decision + cost delta on a real task.
2. **Owned portable context** → no lock-in, leave anytime → run the export, show the
   file, re-import it.
3. **Tested per-model adapters** → switch models without a rewrite → same task, two
   models, one config change.
4. **Self-hosted team harness** → team context stays yours → it runs on your box.

## Objection-handling FAQ

- *"Isn't this just another router?"* No — it's the whole harness (engine + owned
  context + adapters + surfaces). Routing is the wedge; owning your context is the point.
- *"Is GLM 5.2 really good enough?"* For the center of distribution — most knowledge
  work — yes, often better, far cheaper. The router keeps the frontier for the edge.
- *"Why not LangChain/Goose/etc.?"* See [`../docs/WHY-WE-WIN.md`](../docs/WHY-WE-WIN.md):
  none ship automatic cost/capability routing + owned portable context as the product.
- *"Do I need the token?"* No. The software is free and Apache-2.0; the token is culture,
  not a dependency.
- *"Is it production-ready today?"* Read *Current reality* in the README. We don't
  market vaporware.
